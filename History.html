History

Introduction.

The Breakaway with it's sister the "Getaway" were the first two fleets of ships in "Project Breakaway." The
two ships were named through an online contest by Kimberly Powell on September 14, 2011.

Construction of the Norwegian Breakaway began on September 21, 2011 when the first piece of steel was cut in
Papenburg, Germany. On August 25, 2013, the Breakaway was completely assembled. After many inaugural events
she started her transatlantic voyage from Southhampton to New York City where the naming ceremony took place.

New York City became her new home port. And on May 12, 2013, she set sail on her first voyage to the Bermudas.
However, a few months after her departures, several incidents happened, including a woman falling two decks from
her exterior balcony, and toddler fell off of a bunk. Therefore, in 2018, the ship was refurbished.

Now the ship travels to the Bermudas from May through September and the Bahamas October through April.
